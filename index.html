About Me

I work at the intersection of physics, neuroscience, and data science, investigating how complex systems organize, adapt, and undergo disruption. My research integrates computational modeling, network theory, and probabilistic inference to explore dynamic patterns spanning cognition, language, and consciousness.

Raised in a family of artisans, I was trained from an early age to work with symmetries and to learn the art of breaking them with harmony. I bring this sensibility into my scientific work: an eye for detail, a deep perception of nuance, and a mindset that navigates between logic and intuition.

I am driven by intellectual challenges that resist simple answers, problems that demand precision, scientific rigor, and creativity. I enjoy building models, designing metrics, and contributing ideas that help refine and expand theoretical frameworks.
